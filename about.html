About This Temple

Location

The Kalugula Sambhavi Devi Temple, also known as the Ammavari Temple, is located in Sarvayapalem village, Kavali mandal, Nellore district, Andhra Pradesh, India.

History and Legend

According to local legend, the temple's history dates back around 500 years. Kalugula Sambhavi Devi appeared in a dream to the family of farmer Ramireddy in Sarvayapalem village, instructing them to establish a shrine in her honor in a distant place where the sounds of domestic animals like chickens and roosters couldn't reach. Responding to the divine command, the village elders came together to establish the temple, fulfilling the wishes of Kalugula Sambhavi Devi.

Architecture

The temple features traditional Hindu temple architecture, with intricate carvings, ornate sculptures, and vibrant paintings adorning its structure. The sanctum sanctorum houses the idol of Kalugula Sambhavi Devi, which is decorated with floral offerings and adorned by devotees.

Religious Practices

Devotees visit the Kalugula Sambhavi Devi Temple to seek blessings for prosperity, protection, and well-being. Various religious rituals and ceremonies are conducted throughout the year, drawing devotees seeking spiritual solace and guidance.

Community Importance

The temple serves as a focal point for the local community, fostering unity and solidarity among villagers. It plays a central role in religious festivals and cultural celebrations, bringing people together in worship and festivities.
